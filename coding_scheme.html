TWO CODDING SCHEMES

Coding scheme #1

Determine the intervention from SAs to students based on whether a learning task is accomplished by students or SAs

Level Description Code pattern
Questioning oriented (Q) Qa The task of a vignette is accomplished by students themselves through the indirect support of SAs’ guiding questions. SAs may use probing questions before or transition after guiding questions. Little lecturing is used. (pq)-gq-(tr)
Qb The task of a vignette is accomplished by students themselves through the indirect support of SAs’ guiding questions. SAs may use probing questions before or transition after guiding questions. Considerable lecturing is used during this process. However, the information lectured is not directly related to the vignette task. (gq)-(pq)-le-(tr)
Qe Qa and Qb with identifiable errors. er
Direct-instruction oriented (D) Da The task of a vignette is accomplished by SAs through directly imparting to students the content knowledge that they need. SAs may use guiding questions but eventually resort to lecturing to accomplish the vignette task. SAs interact with students during lecturing with the use of probing or checking questions. (pq)-(gq)-le*-pq/cq-(tr)
Db The task of a vignette is accomplished by SAs through directly imparting to students the content knowledge that they need. SAs may use probing questions before or transition/checking questions after lecturing. They barely use any guiding or probing questions during lecturing, but present the content knowledge without paying enough attention to the students’ understanding. (pq)-le*-(cq)
De Da and Db with identifiable errors. er
No intervention (O) O Vignettes where SAs have little impact on students’ learning, which could not represent SAs’ PCK-Q, such as checking students’ progress by asking a sequence of probing questions. (pq)-tr

Coding scheme #2

Determine the intervention from SAs to students based on the presence of guiding questions

Level Description Code pattern
Questioning oriented (Q) Qa The task of a vignette is to help students understand/discover/apply a concept/skill. It is accomplished by the students carrying out a thought process or an experiment supported by a SAs’ guiding questions. It is a student-centered activity and the SA serves in a guiding role. SAs will probably use a sequence of guiding questions interspersed with probing and checking questions. There may be some lecturing, usually very little. pq-gq-(pq/tr/cq)-gq-(pq/tr/cq) very little le
Qb The task of a vignette is to help students understand/discover/apply a concept/skill. The intent is for the task to be carried out by the students themselves, but at somepoint the SA shifts to direct instruction. It begins as a student-centered activity and ends as an instructor centered activity. There will be one or two guiding questions at the beginning, but mostly lecturing after the guiding question(s). (The SA, for example, might ask and answer the quiding question.) There may be checking and probing questions also. (pq/cq)-gq-le- is the general form. This may repeat.
Qe Qa and Qb with identifiable errors. er
Direct-instruction oriented (D) Da The task of a vignette is to help students understand/discover/apply a concept/skill. It is accomplished by direct instruction through lecturing, probing and checking questions. series of pq-le-cq
Db The task of a vignette is to help students understand/discover/apply a concept/skill. It is accomplished by direct instruction through lecturing. The SA does not pay attention to the students' understanding. S/he just transmits information. (cq)-le
De Da and Db with identifiable errors. er
